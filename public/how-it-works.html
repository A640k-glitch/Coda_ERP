What Coda Is For

Coda is an all-in-one system that helps you run your business without jumping between different apps or paper ledgers. Think of it as the brain of your business — it handles your accounts, stock, customers, employees, taxes, and bank records all in one place.

If you run a business in Nigeria — whether it's a provision shop in Mushin, a logistics company in Aba, or a consulting firm in Victoria Island — Coda helps you know how much money you have, who owes you, what you owe, and whether you're making profit or loss. No need for expensive accountants for the small stuff, and no more guessing.

Getting Started

When you first sign up, you'll set up your business profile — your business name, CAC number (if you have one), and what kind of business you run. You'll also pick a plan: Starter, Business Suite, or Enterprise. Don't worry, you get a 14-day free trial to test things out.

Once you're in, Coda creates a set of default accounts for you (like Cash, Bank, Accounts Receivable, etc.) so you can start recording transactions right away. No need to set up a chart of accounts from scratch.

Dashboard Overview

The dashboard is the first screen you see after login. It gives you a quick summary of how your business is doing:

  • Net Income — Your profit after all expenses. This updates as you record sales and expenses.
  • Revenue MTD — How much money came in this month so far.
  • Expenses MTD — What you've spent this month.
  • Expense Breakdown — A chart showing where your money is going (like rent, salaries, supplies).
  • Quick links — Buttons to jump straight to invoicing, adding customers, or recording payments.

Where to input data: The dashboard itself doesn't take data entry — it just shows what you've already entered in other sections. To make the numbers move, go to Accounting, CRM, or Inventory and start entering your records.

Tip: If you see zeros or dashes, it just means there's no data yet. Once you add transactions, the numbers will fill in.

Accounting

This is where the core of your financial records live. The Accounting section has:

  • General Ledger — The master record of every financial transaction in your business. Think of it as the main book where everything is written down.
  • Accounts — Your individual account balances (Cash, Bank, Accounts Receivable, Accounts Payable, etc.). Each account shows its current balance.
  • Journal Entries — Every transaction you record creates a journal entry. This is how money moves from one account to another (e.g., when a customer pays you, money moves from "Accounts Receivable" to "Cash").
  • Invoices — Create and send invoices to your customers. When you create an invoice, it automatically records the amount in Accounts Receivable.
  • Payments — Record when customers pay you. This moves money from Accounts Receivable to Cash/Bank.
  • Expenses — Record your business expenses. Categorize them so you can see where your money goes.

How data flows: You create an invoice → it adds to Accounts Receivable → customer pays → you record payment → money moves to your Cash/Bank account. All of this is automatically reflected in your Profit & Loss and Balance Sheet reports.

Inventory

If you sell physical products, you need to track what you have in stock. The Inventory section lets you:

  • Add products — Give each product a name, description, price, and quantity.
  • Track stock levels — See how many units you have left at any time.
  • Record stock movements — When goods come in (purchase) or go out (sale), update the quantities.
  • Multiple depots — If you have more than one shop or warehouse, you can track stock in each location separately.

Where to input: Add your products under the Inventory tab. When you make a sale that includes products, the inventory automatically reduces. When you buy new stock, you add to the quantity.

CRM & Customers

This section helps you keep track of everyone you do business with — your customers and suppliers.

  • Customer Profiles — Store names, phone numbers, email addresses, and transaction history for each customer.
  • Transaction History — See every invoice and payment related to a customer in one place.
  • Contact Management — Keep notes on each customer, like their preferred payment method or important dates.
  • Communication Log — Record calls, emails, or visits with customers so your team is always on the same page.

Where to input: When you first start, add your existing customers manually. After that, every time you create an invoice for someone new, you can add them on the spot.

HR & Payroll

If you have employees, this section helps you manage them and pay them correctly.

  • Employee Records — Store each employee's details, job title, salary, and bank account.
  • Payroll Processing — Calculate salaries, deductions (PAYE tax, pension), and net pay for each employee.
  • Payslips — Generate printable payslips for your employees.
  • Leave Management — Track annual leave, sick days, and other time off.

How it works: Add each employee with their salary details. When it's time to pay, run the payroll process — Coda calculates PAYE tax based on Nigerian tax brackets, deducts pension contributions, and shows you the net amount each employee should receive. The payroll entries are automatically recorded in your accounting ledger.

Tax Center

Nigerian businesses have to deal with multiple taxes — VAT, WHT, CIT, PAYE. The Tax Center helps you keep track and stay compliant with FIRS.

  • VAT (7.5%) — Automatically calculated on your sales and purchases. You can see how much VAT you've collected and how much you can claim back.
  • WHT (Withholding Tax) — Track WHT deducted from your payments and WHT deducted from customers who pay you.
  • PAYE — Employee income tax calculated based on Nigerian tax brackets.
  • CIT (Company Income Tax) — Track your taxable income for annual filing.
  • Tax Reports — Generate reports showing your tax liabilities for each period.

Where to input: The tax calculations are mostly automatic based on what you enter in Accounting and HR. Just make sure your transactions are correctly categorized and your employee salaries are set up properly.

Bank Reconciliation

Bank reconciliation is how you make sure the money in your Coda records matches what's actually in your bank account. This is one of the most important things you can do to keep your accounts accurate.

  • Upload Bank Statement — Upload your bank statement (CSV or PDF) and Coda will try to match each transaction with what you've recorded.
  • Auto-Match — Coda compares your bank transactions against your recorded payments and invoices, and flags matches.
  • Manual Review — For transactions that don't match automatically, you can manually match them or create new entries.
  • Reconciliation Summary — Shows how many transactions are matched, unmatched, and your match rate percentage.

How it works: Download your bank statement from your internet banking, upload it to Coda, and review the matches. Once you're satisfied, confirm the reconciliation. This keeps your book balance and bank balance in sync.

Reports

Reports help you understand your business performance at a glance. You don't need to be an accountant to read them.

  • Profit & Loss (P&L) — Shows your income minus expenses over a period. This tells you if you're making profit or loss.
  • Balance Sheet — Shows what you own (assets), what you owe (liabilities), and what belongs to you (equity) at a specific date.
  • Trial Balance — A list of all your accounts and their balances. Used to make sure your books are balanced.
  • Tax Reports — Summary of your tax liabilities for filing.
  • Export to CSV/PDF — Download any report for your accountant or for filing with FIRS/CAC.

Where to input: Reports are generated automatically from the data you enter in Accounting, HR, and Inventory. You don't input anything directly in Reports — it's your window into what's already in the system.

Integrations & API Access

Once API access is live for your account, you'll be able to connect Coda to your bank, payment gateways, and other tools you already use. This means less manual typing and fewer mistakes.

What you'll be able to connect:

  • Bank Accounts (via Mono) — Connect your business bank account to automatically import transactions. No more manual entry of bank statements.
  • CAC Verification (via QoreID) — Instantly verify your customers' CAC registration numbers when onboarding them.
  • SMS Notifications (via Termii) — Send payment reminders, invoice alerts, and OTPs directly to your customers' phones.
  • Tax Filing (via FIRS/TaxPromax) — File VAT, WHT, and other tax returns directly from Coda without logging into multiple portals.
  • Payment Gateways (Paystack, Flutterwave) — Accept card payments, bank transfers, and USSD payments from your customers.

What you need to do to prepare:

  1. Sign up for developer accounts with the providers listed above (Mono, QoreID, Termii, etc.). Most of them have free tiers or pay-as-you-go plans.
  2. Get your API keys from each provider's dashboard.
  3. Once Coda's integration settings page is ready, you'll paste those keys into your account settings and the connections will work automatically.
  4. Open the API documentation → to see the full technical details, including endpoint URLs, request formats, and code examples, and test them with our live interactive API tester.

Enterprise API Integrations

Integrate Coda's real-time banking, compliance, and messaging modules. To use these endpoints, supply the respective API keys in your backend environment.

POST Verify CAC Registration

/api/v1/integrations/cac/verify

Query CAC registry to validate business numbers (RC/BN).

Field Type Description
rcNumber string CAC number (e.g. RC1234567)
companyName string Optional. Registered name for cross-check

POST Sync Bank Statement (Mono)

/api/v1/integrations/bank/sync

Fetch real-time transaction feeds from connected Nigerian bank accounts for auto-reconciliation.

Field Type Description
accountId string Mono connected account ID
period string Date range e.g., last30days

POST Automated Tax Filing (FIRS)

/api/v1/integrations/tax/file

Remit VAT/WHT directly to TaxPromax.

Field Type Description
tin string FIRS Tax Identification Number
period string Tax period e.g. 2026-06
amount number Total tax amount (NGN)

POST Send SMS via Termii

/api/v1/integrations/notifications/sms

Send high-deliverability SMS for OTPs and payment reminders.

Field Type Description
to string Recipient Phone (e.g. 23480...)
message string SMS Content
